Friedrich Schröder (August 6, 1910 – September 25, 1972) was a German composer of what could be referred to as light music . Born in Näfels , Switzerland , Schröder studied music in Stuttgart , Münster , and Berlin . Already in those early days he wrote film scores . For a short period between 1934 and 1937 he also worked as a Kapellmeister . He composed a number of operettas and Schlager , many of which have been popular ever since. Schröder died in West Berlin, aged 62.
