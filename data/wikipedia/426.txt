Hubert Charles Bath (6 November 1883 – 24 April 1945) was a British film composer, music director, and conductor.  His credits include Tudor Rose (1936), A Yank at Oxford (1938) and Millions Like Us (1943). Bath was born in Barnstaple , Devon in 1883. In 1924, Bath was named as co-respondent in the divorce case between Colonel Alfred Rawlinson and the actress Jean Aylwin .  His composition Out of the Blue has been used as the theme music of Sports Report since the programme started in 1948.  Also well-known is his Cornish Rhapsody , written for, and essential to the plot of, the 1944 film Love Story . He died in Harefield , Middlesex in 1945, aged 61. His son John Bath (1915–2004) was also a film composer.
