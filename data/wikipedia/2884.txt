Jakob Rosenhain ( Jacob , Jacques ) (2 December 1813 in Mannheim  â€“ 21 March 1894 in Baden-Baden )  was a Jewish and German pianist and composer of classical music. Rosenhain made his debut at the age of 11.  During their 1837 season, he was a soloist with the London Philharmonic Orchestra (on April 17), which in 1854 (also in April) programmed one of his symphonies.   He worked with Johann Baptist Cramer on a published school of piano-playing. From 1849 he made his home in Paris.  He was a friend of Felix Mendelssohn at least from 1839.
