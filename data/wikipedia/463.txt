Max Josef Beer (August 25, 1851 - 25 November 1908 in Vienna ) was an Austrian composer. On 15 February 1871, his opera seria in four acts, "Elizabeth of Hungary," premiered at Milan's La Scala , starring Elisabetta Sternberg (soprano), Louis Auguste Arsandaux (tenor), Jean-Louis Lasalle (baritone) and Joseph-Victor Warot (bass). Beer also wrote "Der Streik der Schmiede," a realist one act opera, which unsuccessfully premiered at Augsburg, 1897.
