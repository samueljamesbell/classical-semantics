Antonín Kammel (April 21, 1730 – 5 October 1784 or 1785) was a composer and violinist . His best-known composition is String Quartet no. 2. Kammel was born at Běleč, now in the Czech Republic .  He was part of the social circle of the leading 18th century London concert impresarios, Johann Christian Bach and Carl Friedrich Abel . He also performed at provincial musical festivals, leading the orchestra at Salisbury in 1771.  He died in London in 1784/5.
