Léon Orthel (4 October 1905, Roosendaal – 6 September 1985, The Hague )  was a Dutch composer, pianist and teacher. In 1921 he became a student of the Royal Conservatory of The Hague . He studied violin with André Spoor,  piano with Everhard van Beijnum and composition with Johan Wagenaar . He later studied at the Berlin Hochschule für Musik with Paul Juon and Curt Sachs .  His compositions include among other works 6 symphonies   and two cello concertos.   His third and fifth symphonies received awards from the Dutch government, in 1946 and 1962 respectively.
