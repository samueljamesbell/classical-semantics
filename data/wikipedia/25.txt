Kornél Ábrányi (15 October 1822 – 20 December 1903) was a Hungarian pianist , music writer and theorist , and composer . He was born in Szentgyörgyábrány . A pupil of Frédéric Chopin , and a close friend of Franz Liszt , whose music he championed, Ábrányi chiefly wrote music for piano , but also composed chamber music , choral works, and lieder . He began teaching at the Franz Liszt Academy of Music at its founding in 1875 and became its Secretary. He was one of several people to use the pseudonym Kákay Aranyos  . Ábrányi died in Budapest , aged 81. His grandson was the composer Emil Ábrányi .
