Sven-Erik Bäck (16 September 1919 – 10 January 1994) was a Swedish composer of classical music . He was born in Stockholm . Bäck studied from 1939 until 1943 in the King's Music-Academy and from 1940 until 1945, was a composition student of Hilding Rosenberg .  He travelled in 1951 to have further studies with Goffredo Petrassi in Rome. As of 1953 he was the leader of the chamber orchestra of the Swedish Radio Orchestra. He was also a member of string quartets - the Kyndel Quartet from 1940 to 1944  and the Barkel Quartet from 1944 to 1953. Bäck composed three operas , five works for ballet , many concertos , a number of works for chamber ensemble including at least four string quartets , an oratorio, cantatas , choral works, lieder and music for plays and film . He died in Stockholm in 1994.
