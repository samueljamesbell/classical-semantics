Gioacchino Cocchi (ca. 1720â€“1804) was an Italian composer . He was particularly famous for his theatre music (he wrote approximately fifty operas ). His first works were performed at Naples and Rome . From 1750 to 1757 he stayed in Venice , where he became chapel master of the Ospedale degli Incurabili (from 1752). He also taught composition to Andrea Luchesi (1756/57). In 1757 he traveled to London , where he stayed till 1772. He came back to Venice in 1773.
