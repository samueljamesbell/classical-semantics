Thomas Adams (5 September 1785 â€“ 15 September 1858) was an English organist and composer for organ. Born in London , Adams studied under Thomas Busby and served as organist at several prominent London churches. His published organ works include many fugues , voluntaries, ninety interludes , and several variations on popular airs . He also wrote variations for piano and many anthems , hymns , and sacred songs. He was known for his skill in improvisation and for years was in charge of performances on Flight & Robson's Apollonicon .
