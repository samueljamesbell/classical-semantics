Jacob Raphael Druckman (June 26,  1928 – May 24, 1996) was an American composer born in Philadelphia . A graduate of the Juilliard School , Druckman studied with Vincent Persichetti , Peter Mennin , and Bernard Wagenaar . In 1949 and 1950 he studied with Aaron Copland at Tanglewood and later continued his studies at the École Normale de Musique in Paris (1954–55). He worked extensively with electronic music , in addition to a number of works for orchestra or for small ensembles. In 1972 he won the Pulitzer Prize for his first large orchestral work, Windows .  He was composer-in-residence of the New York Philharmonic from 1982 until 1985. Druckman taught at Juilliard, The Aspen Music Festival , Tanglewood, Brooklyn College , Bard College , and Yale University , among other appointments. He is Connecticut's State Composer Laureate.  Druckman died of lung cancer at age 67. His music is published by Boosey & Hawkes. He is the father of percussionist Daniel Druckman. Notable musicians who have recorded his works include David Zinman , Wolfgang Sawallisch , Zubin Mehta , Leonard Slatkin , Dawn Upshaw , Jan DeGaetani , Dorian Wind Quintet , and the American Brass Quintet .
