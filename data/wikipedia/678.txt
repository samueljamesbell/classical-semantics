Modesta Bor ( Juan Griego , Isla de Margarita , 1926 – Mérida , 1998) was a Venezuelan compositor. Modesta Bor was born in Juan Griego, Isla de Margarita. She studied in Caracas with Elena Arrarte , Juan Bautista Plaza , Antonio Estévez , Maria de Lourdes Rotundo and Vicente Emilio Sojo , graduating with a degree in composition in 1959. She continued her studies at the Moscow Tchaikovsky Conservatory with Aram Khachaturian .  In 1960 she won her first National Music Prize with Sonata for violin and piano . After completing her studies, Bor returned to Venezuela to work as a composer, teacher and choir director, becoming head of the music department in the Central University of Venezuela Culture Department. She also served as director of the musicology section of Folklore Research of the National Service. She died in Mérida .
