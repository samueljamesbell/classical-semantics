Francesco Maria Bazzani or Bassani (c. 1650 â€“ c. 1700) was an Italian baroque composer. Francesco was a member of the musical Bassani family, of whom Giovanni Battista Bassani is the best remembered today. He is to be distinguished from another Francesco Maria Bassani two generations earlier ( fl. 1621), nephew of the viola da gamba composer Oratio Bassani , who kept a pedagogic notebook Regole di contrapunto , which contains eight pieces, seven of which are probably by his uncle Oratio. He was maestro di cappella of the Duomo di Piacenza from 1673  Upon the death of his sister and her husband, a musician named Keller from Germany, Francesco adopted his nephew Fortunato Chelleri and trained him as a musician.
