Henriette van den Boorn-Coclet (15 Jan 1866 – 6 March 1945) was a Belgian composer. She was born in Liege , Belgium and studied at the Liège Conservatory with Jean-Théodore Radoux and Sylvain Dupuis , where she received a first prize in solfège (1887) harmony (1882) and fugue (1884). She also won a silver medal for chamber music (piano and strings) in 1886.  After completing her education, she took a position teaching harmony at the Conservatory (1892–1931).  She won the Prix de Rome in 1895.  She died in Liege. Her compositions make use of a late 19th-century neo-romantic style.  Boorn-Coclet composed for orchestra, chamber ensemble, songs and piano.
